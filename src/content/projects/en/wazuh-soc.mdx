## The starting point

A growing multi-branch business with **400+ critical assets**, no centralized log collection, no correlation layer, and no real visibility into what was happening on its endpoints, servers or perimeter. Incident response was reactive — driven by tickets, not telemetry.

## What I built

**1. Wazuh-based SOC**

- Centralized log ingestion across Windows servers, Linux infra, network appliances and endpoints
- Correlation rules tuned for the actual threat model — not generic noise
- ~**80% visibility** on critical assets within the first year
- Dashboards and on-call workflows that made the data actionable

**2. Infrastructure pentest**

- Internal-perspective pentest covering network, AD, endpoints and exposed services
- **16 critical vulnerabilities** identified, with severity-driven remediation roadmap
- Worked with infra/dev teams to close them — not just hand over a PDF

**3. Attack-surface reduction**

- **Zero Trust architecture** rolled out across branches
- **Cloudflare WAF** in front of public-facing services
- Hardening sweep of Linux/Windows infrastructure
- IAM governance + privileged access controls

## The result

A company that previously had no detection layer ran with a working SOC, a documented incident response process, and a measurable security posture. The 16 critical findings were closed; the SOC became the steady-state operation, not the project.

## What I learned

Visibility without **prioritization** is just more noise. The win wasn't the SIEM — it was the prioritized remediation roadmap that made the SIEM's output actionable.
